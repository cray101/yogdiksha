Samādhi

Samādhi (समाधि) is the final stage of the path of Yoga. It may be roughly translated as liberation. Through constant practice of meditation, the mind unites with the source of creation.

The individual soul (ātman) fuses with the cosmic consciousness (brahman)

तदेवार्थमात्रनिर्भासं स्वरूपशून्यमिव समाधिः॥३॥

PYS 3.3: Tadeva Arthamātranirbhāsam Svarūpaśūnyam Iva Samādhih  

when only the essence of the object shines forth in the mind, as if devoid of it’s own form, 

that state of deep absorption is Samadhi. 

  • •tad = that
  • •eva = the same
  • •artha = object, place, point
  • •matra = only, alone
  • •nirbhasam = shines forth, appears
  • •svarupa = own form, own nature (sva = own; rupe = form, nature)
  • •shunyam = devoid of, empty
  • •iva = as if, as it were
  • •samadhih = meditation in its higher state, deep absorption of meditation, the state of perfected concentration, spiritual absorption
