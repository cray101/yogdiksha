Eight Limbs of Yoga

योगाङ्गाऽनुष्ठानादशुद्धिक्षये ज्ञानदीप्तिराविवेकख्यातेः॥२८॥

PYS 2.28: Yoga anga anuṣṭhanad aśuddhi kśaye jñana dīptih a viveka khyāteḥ

through the practice of the different limbs of yoga the impurities are eliminated and the light of the eternal wisdom is free to shine 

यमनियमासनप्राणायामप्रत्याहारधारणाध्यानसमाधयोऽष्टावङ्गानि॥२९॥

PYS 2.29: yama niyama āsana prānāyāma pratyāhāra dhāranā dhyāna samādhi aṣtau angani

the eight limbs of yoga are: Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, 

Dharana, Dhyana and Samadhi

The second chapter of the Yoga Sūtras (Sādhana Pāda) defines Yoga as made up of eight parts (Āṣṭanga). The eight limbs of Yoga describe the different facets of the Yogic practice and gives us direction on the path.

The stages go from gross to subtle, and from outwards to inwards. There is a gradual ascent from one state to the next, however, the states often overlap, and we can catch glimpses of one in the other.
